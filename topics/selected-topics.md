## Topic 1: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists — Clinical Data vs. Market Hype

**Research Prompt:**
"Analyze Phase 2 and Phase 3 clinical trial data from 2023 to present evaluating GLP-1 receptor agonists for two specific non-weight-loss indications: Alzheimer's disease and alcohol/substance addiction. For each indication, contrast the actual peer-reviewed efficacy data and reported adverse event rates against mainstream media narratives and corporate pipeline projections. Identify where the evidence is strong, where it is preliminary, and where media coverage has outpaced the science."

**Tests:** Medical evidence synthesis, conflict resolution (science vs. hype), source quality (journals vs. press), epistemic honesty

## Topic 2: Semiconductor Supply Chain Restructuring

**Research Prompt:**
"Investigate how the global semiconductor supply chain has restructured since 2020, focusing on specific chokepoints: advanced packaging (CoWoS, HBM), leading-edge fab capacity (TSMC, Samsung, Intel), and critical materials (neon, rare gases, photoresists). Track firm-level capital expenditure decisions and government subsidy programs (CHIPS Act, EU Chips Act, Japan/South Korea incentives). What are the strategic implications for AI accelerator hardware availability and pricing through 2027?"

**Tests:** Geopolitical synthesis, quantitative data (CapEx figures, capacity numbers), recency, firm-level primary sources

## Topic 3: Data Center Energy Constraints and Hyperscale Expansion

**Research Prompt:**
"Investigate the energy grid constraints and zoning policy conflicts surrounding hyperscale data center expansion in Northern Virginia (Loudoun and Prince William counties) from 2022 to present. Include specific utility filings, PJM grid capacity data, and local government decisions. Compare this situation to the incentive packages and grid readiness of emerging secondary markets (Ohio, Texas, the Nordics) competing for AI compute facilities. What is the realistic outlook for new AI data center capacity through 2028?"

**Tests:** Hyper-local document retrieval, infrastructure analysis, quantitative grid data, operational reality vs. corporate announcements

## Topic 4: Global Insect Population Decline

**Research Prompt:**
"Evaluate the leading scientific theories explaining the reported global decline in insect populations (habitat loss, pesticide exposure, climate change, light pollution, invasive species). For each proposed cause, assess the quality and consistency of the supporting evidence, including key studies, their methodological strengths and limitations, and where expert consensus exists versus where findings are contested. Which conservation interventions have the strongest evidence of effectiveness?"

**Tests:** Ecological science literacy, evidence quality assessment, epistemic honesty (contested causation), source quality (peer-reviewed ecology journals)

---

## Topic 5: Utility-Scale Battery Storage Technology Comparison

**Research Prompt:**
"Compare lithium iron phosphate (LFP), nickel manganese cobalt (NMC), sodium-ion, and flow battery technologies for utility-scale grid storage applications. For each chemistry, evaluate: safety record and fire incident data, levelized cost of storage (current and projected to 2030), cycle life and degradation characteristics, supply chain dependencies, and deployment suitability across different grid contexts (US, EU, China, India). Which technology is best positioned for which use case, and where do expert assessments diverge?"

**Tests:** Technical depth (electrochemistry), cross-format evidence (patents, standards, incident reports, market data), cross-jurisdiction comparison, quantitative cost analysis
